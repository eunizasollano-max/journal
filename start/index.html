why I made it

I've been journaling since I was a kid. Then I was diagnosed with PTSD and depression, and I stopped — because most days, I didn't know what to write. How do you write about a day where you didn't do anything but just exist? It felt like there was nothing worth putting down.

I made this for my younger self. To tell her that even on the darkest days, it's okay to just exist — that showing up to write one honest line, even "today I just existed," still counts.

I wanted something that felt like a good voice talking back to me, so I made it kind to the eyes, easy to navigate, and all-in-one — so on the days you have nothing left, you're not also fighting five different apps just to get a thought down.

— Yuni

what it is

A private, all-in-one journal that meets you wherever you are that day — including days where "wherever you are" is just barely here. Track your mood, write freely or follow a gentle prompt, rate your day, drop in a photo, set small goals or routines if you want structure.

There's a verse each day too — not as a lesson, just something good to read, the kind of thing that's nice to hear whether or not it's "for" you. Everything lives in one place, on purpose: when you're already low on energy, switching between five different apps is its own kind of exhausting.

how it's different
Go at your own pace
Missed a week? Go back and fill in the days you skipped, whenever you're ready. Turn off the streak counter if it feels like pressure instead of encouragement.
Free write or guided
Some days you know exactly what to say. Other days you need a question to start with. Both are built in — switch depending on what you have in you.
Morning ≠ evening
Your 7am self and your 9pm self aren't the same person — so the prompts change with the time of day.
Look back, not just forward
Every month recaps itself — your average mood, your average day rating, the patterns you'd never notice day-to-day — and the gallery holds onto the little photo memories you dropped in along the way.
